    PS10-3

    • Due Nov 17 by 11:59pm
    • Points 0
    • Available until Nov 20 at 11:59pm
    This assignment was locked Nov 20 at 11:59pm.

    This problem is canceled.

    You are given the following points in the plane, (x1,y1) through (x7,y7):

    (1, 3),  (2, 5),  (3, 7),  (5, 11),  (7, 14),  (8, 15),  (10, 19)

    You want to find a line y = mx + b that approximately passes through these points (no line is a perfect fit). Write a linear program to find the line that minimizes the maximum absolute error:

        max  | yi - (mxi + b) |
         1≤i≤7

    For example, if m = 1 and b = 1, the errors for the seven points above are:

    (1,3):    1
    (2,5):    2
    (3,7):    3
    (5,11):   5
    (7,14):   6
    (8,15):   6
    (10,19):  8

    The maximum absolute error is 8.  Is that the smallest that we can make the error?  (Probably not.)  What values of m and b would yield the lowest maximum absolute error?  (That's the point of this problem.)

    Use your linear program to calculate the optimal values for m and b as well as the maximum absolute error.  You can find various linear program solvers on the web.  I used the LPSolve IDE in class.

    Hand in a one-page PDF document (we will not accept documents containing handwritten or hand drawn material) that contains

    1. Your linear program
    2. Screen shot(s) showing your program entered into a solver as well as your program's solution
    3. The optimal values for m, b, and maximum absolute error calculated by the solver.

    There's no need to be wordy.  Just the clearly presented facts, please.
